SatDump is a free, open-source satellite data processing application. Whether you want to receive live weather imagery from NOAA and Meteor satellites, decode telemetry from scientific missions, or process large X-band basebands, SatDump handles the entire pipeline — from raw IQ samples to calibrated image products.

## Getting started in 3 steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install SatDump">
    Download the latest release for your platform from the [GitHub Releases page](https://github.com/SatDump/SatDump/releases), or follow the [build instructions](/installation) for your OS.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your SDR or load a baseband">
    Plug in your RTL-SDR, Airspy, HackRF, or other supported device for live processing — or load an existing baseband file for offline decoding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a pipeline and start decoding">
    Choose the satellite pipeline matching your target (e.g., `noaa_apt`, `meteor_m2_lrpt`, `goes_hrit`) and press **Start**. SatDump processes the signal and saves the image products automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  SatDump supports 20+ SDR devices and 100+ satellite pipelines covering weather satellites, scientific missions, geostationary imagers, and more. See the [pipeline reference](/reference/pipelines-overview) for the full list.
</Note>
