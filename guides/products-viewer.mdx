After a pipeline finishes decoding, SatDump automatically organises the resulting satellite products in the **Root** panel and opens them for viewing. You can apply image composites, add geographic overlays, and export the results in several image formats — all without leaving the GUI.

## Accessing products

Once a pipeline completes, the decoded products appear in the **Root** under the satellite name. Expand the tree to find individual instruments. Selecting an instrument opens its view in the right pane.

<Info>
  Products are added automatically when the **Automatically Process Products** setting is enabled (the default). You can toggle this in **File > Settings** under **General Settings**.
</Info>

## Working with imaging instruments

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a preset or write an expression">
    With an imaging instrument selected, open the **Presets** dropdown to choose a predefined composite (for example, a false-colour or natural-colour view). Alternatively, type a custom expression directly into the **Expression** field to combine individual channels.

    Image expressions use a simple syntax to blend and manipulate channels. See [Image expressions](/advanced/image-expressions) for a full reference.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply the composite">
    Click **Apply** below the **Expression** field. The right pane updates to show the composite image.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add map overlays (optional)">
    Select **Current > Add Overlay** from the menu bar, then click **Apply** again. Overlays include country borders and city labels projected onto the image.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the image">
    Select **Current > Save Image** from the menu bar to export the current view to disk. The output filename follows the template configured in **File > Settings**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Output image formats

Set the default format in **File > Settings** under **Product Image Format** (for raw instrument images) and **Processed Image Format** (for composites and processed outputs).

| Format | Extension | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| PNG | `.png` | Recommended. Lossless, widely compatible. |
| TIFF | `.tif`, `.tiff` | Lossless; preferred for GIS workflows. |
| JPEG 2000 | `.j2k` | Lossless or lossy; large files. |
| JPEG | `.jpg`, `.jpeg` | Lossy; not recommended — may degrade composites and projections. |
| PBM | `.pbm`, `.pgm` | Very fast write; very large files. |
| QOI | `.qoi` | Fast with compression; 8-bit only. |

<Warning>
  JPEG compression introduces artefacts that can affect the quality of composites and projections. Use PNG or TIFF unless file size is a hard constraint.
</Warning>

## Image filename templates

SatDump generates output filenames from a configurable template. The default template is `$i_$c` (instrument name + composite name). You can switch to a more detailed template or define your own in **File > Settings** under **Image Filename Template**.

See [Image filename template](/advanced/image-filename-template) for the full list of available variables.

## Adding products to a projection

To reproject an image onto a geographic map, click **Add to Proj** in the instrument view. SatDump adds the current composite or channel to a new **Projection** item in the **Others** section of the **Root**. See [Projections](/guides/projections) for the full workflow.
