SatDump supports a wide range of low-Earth-orbit (LEO) polar-orbiting weather satellite missions, from the classic NOAA APT audio broadcasts to the high-resolution X-band dumps of modern operational satellites. Each accordion section below lists all pipeline IDs for that mission, along with every configurable parameter.

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="NOAA">

    ### `noaa_hrpt` — NOAA HRPT

    Produces AVHRR imagery and ancillary channel data from the High Resolution Picture Transmission downlink.

    No additional parameters.

    ---

    ### `noaa_gac` — NOAA GAC

    Processes the Global Area Coverage reduced-resolution AVHRR data stream.

    **Parameters:**

    - `backward` — Over the US, NOAA satellites often transmit in reverse order. Set to `true` to decode those backward replays correctly.

    ---

    ### `noaa_dsb` — NOAA DSB

    Processes the Direct Sounder Broadcast (DSB) data stream.

    No additional parameters.

    ---

    ### `noaa_apt` — NOAA APT

    Decodes the Automatic Picture Transmission analogue image broadcast. Produces false-colour and channel-A/B imagery.

    **Parameters:**

    - `satellite_number` — Satellite number. Required for projections and overlays. Options: `15`, `18`, `19`.
    - `start_timestamp` — Unix timestamp (UTC seconds) of the recording start. Required for projections and overlays. If your `.wav` file is a supported format the timestamp will be read automatically.
    - `align_timestamps` — Aligns the pass to the correct time using timing marks in the APT space data. The start time must still be within 15 seconds for this to work. Default: `true`.
    - `sdrpp_noise_reduction` — Applies the APT noise reduction originally implemented in SDR++. Default: `true`.
    - `save_unsynced` — Saves the image before synchronization. Useful for weak signals or narrowband receivers. Default: `true`.
    - `autocrop_wedges` — Automatically crops the image to include only telemetry wedges considered valid. May discard a lot of data on poor passes. Default: `false`.
    - `max_crop_stddev` — When `autocrop_wedges` is enabled, sets the amount of noise to allow at the top/bottom of the image. Lower values produce more aggressive cropping. Default: `3500`. Useful range: approximately `400`–`10000`.
    - `save_wav` — Saves the intermediate WAV file. Default: `false`.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Meteor M">

    ### `meteor_hrpt` — METEOR HRPT

    Produces MSU-MR imagery from the Meteor-M HRPT downlink.

    **Parameters:**

    - `year_override` — Overrides the year used for timestamps. Required when you process a file in a different year than the data was created.

    ---

    ### `meteor_m2_lrpt` — METEOR M2 LRPT 72k

    Decodes the Low-Resolution Picture Transmission broadcast from Meteor-M2 at 72 kbit/s. Produces MSU-MR RGB imagery.

    **Parameters:**

    - `satellite_number` — If specified, overrides automatic satellite detection and treats the data as the given satellite. Valid options: `"M2"`, `"M2-2"`, `"M2-3"`, `"M2-4"`.
    - `fill_missing` — Fills in black lines caused by signal drop-outs or interference.
    - `max_fill_lines` — Maximum number of contiguous lines to fill in. Default: `50`.

    ---

    ### `meteor_m2-x_lrpt` — METEOR M2-X LRPT 72k

    Same as `meteor_m2_lrpt`, with an additional Reed-Solomon check parameter.

    **Parameters:**

    All parameters from `meteor_m2_lrpt`, plus:

    - `rs_usecheck` — Discards data that fails the Reed-Solomon check. Recommended to leave enabled. Set to `false` only if the satellite is transmitting bad RS codes. Default: `true`.

    ---

    ### `meteor_m2-x_lrpt_80k` — METEOR M2-x LRPT 80k

    Decodes the LRPT broadcast at 80 kbit/s.

    **Parameters:**

    Same parameters as `meteor_m2_lrpt` (`satellite_number`, `fill_missing`, `max_fill_lines`).

    ---

    ### `meteor_m_dump_narrow` — METEOR-M Narrow Dump

    <Warning>This pipeline is a work in progress (WIP) and may not produce complete results.</Warning>

    No additional parameters.

    ---

    ### `meteor_m_dump_wide` — METEOR-M Wide Dump

    <Warning>This pipeline is a work in progress (WIP) and may not produce complete results.</Warning>

    No additional parameters.

  <Accordion title="MetOp">

    ### `metop_ahrpt` — MetOp AHRPT

    Processes the MetOp Advanced HRPT downlink. Produces AVHRR/3, AMSU, MHS, and ancillary data products.

    **Parameters:**

    - `write_hpt` — Generate a `.hpt` file suitable for loading into HRPT Reader for further processing.

    ---

    ### `metop_dump` — MetOp X-Band dump

    Processes the MetOp X-band high-rate direct broadcast dump. Produces the full instrument suite.

    No additional parameters.
